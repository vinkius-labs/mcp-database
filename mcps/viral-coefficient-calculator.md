## Description
The Viral Coefficient Calculator is a specialized tool for analyzing product virality. By using the `evaluate_virality` tool, you can determine your K-factor and growth classification (Sub-critical, Critical, or Viral). Use `estimate_doubling_period` to find out how many cycles are needed to double your user base, and `forecast_growth_trajectory` to project population expansion over multiple future cycles. This MCP connects AI agents directly to precise viral growth modeling.

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: What is the K-factor?**
The K-factor (Viral Coefficient) represents the average number of new users generated by a single existing user. It is calculated by multiplying the average number of invitations sent per user by the conversion rate of those invitees.

**Q: How do I know if my product is viral?**
A K-factor greater than 1 indicates exponential growth (Viral). A K-factor of exactly 1 represents steady, linear growth (Critical), and a K-factor below 1 means the viral loop will eventually stop without external input (Sub-critical).

**Q: Can I project future user growth?**
Yes, by using the `forecast_growth_trajectory` tool, you can simulate and view the projected total user population for a specific number of future growth cycles.
