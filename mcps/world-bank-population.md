## Overview

**Category:** [the-unthinkable](../categories/the-unthinkable.md)

Access demographic data, total populations, poverty headcount, and inequality (Gini) statistics instantly from the World Bank.

## Description
Give your AI agent comprehensive knowledge of global demographics and social data.

### What you can do
- **Demographics** — Track total, urban, and rural populations.
- **Growth Rates** — Access reliable year-over-year population growth trends.
- **Poverty & Inequality** — Investigate GINI indexes and poverty ratios.

### How it works
1. Subscribe to the server.
2. Skip the auth configuration.
3. Make your AI agent a global demographer.

### Who is this for?
NGOs, sociologists, government researchers, and urban planners.


## Available Tools
- **get_total_population**: Get total population
- **get_population_growth**: Get annual population growth percentage
- **get_poverty**: 15 a day at 2017 international prices.

Get poverty headcount ratio at .15 a day
- **get_gini_index**: Get Gini index
- **get_social_indicator**: Get any World Bank popupation/social indicator by code


## 💬 Prompt Examples

Here are some examples of how you can interact with the **World Bank Population** MCP server using an AI Agent (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.).

**👤 You:**
> "What is the total population and Gini index of Brazil?"

**🤖 AI Agent:**
> 🌍 **Brazil: Population & Inequality**

Brazil has a population of approximately 215 million people and a Gini index of 0.53, indicating significant income inequality — one of the highest in Latin America.

---

**👤 You:**
> "Compare the urbanization rate of China and India over the last 20 years."

**🤖 AI Agent:**
> 🌍 **Urbanization: China vs India (2004–2024)**

China's urban population surged from 41% to over 65%, driven by massive infrastructure investment. India's urbanization grew more gradually from 29% to 36%, reflecting its still largely rural economy.

---

**👤 You:**
> "Which countries in Sub-Saharan Africa have made the most progress in reducing extreme poverty?"

**🤖 AI Agent:**
> 🌍 **Poverty Reduction: Sub-Saharan Africa**

Ethiopia and Rwanda stand out with significant reductions in extreme poverty over the past two decades, moving millions above the $2.15/day threshold through sustained economic growth.
