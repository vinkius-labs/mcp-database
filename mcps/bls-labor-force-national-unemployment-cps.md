## Description
The official National Unemployment tracking MCP powered by the BLS Current Population Survey (CPS).

### What you can do
- **National Unemployment Rate** — Track the exact headline figure released by the US Government.
- **Labor Force Participation** — Measure how many working-age adults are actively employed or seeking work.
- **Demographic Breakdowns** — Analyze unemployment across specific cohorts (e.g., age, gender, education).

### How it works
1. Sign up for a free BLS API Key
2. Define it in your settings
3. Have your AI monitor the health of the US workforce in real-time.


## Available Tools
- **get_unemployment_rate**: S. unemployment figure generated by the Current Population Survey. Crucial for understanding labor force tightness and workforce health.

Get the National Unemployment Rate (LNS14000000) from the CPS
- **query_bls**: Use this instead of specific endpoints if you intimately know the underlying numerical code. Up to 50 concurrent lookbacks allowed.

Generic BLS v2 api timeseries query. Requires explicit BLS Series IDs

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: How do I start?**
Input your free API Key into the configuration and simply ask your AI for unemployment percentages. The system builds the proper BLS query automatically.

**Q: Is this updated monthly?**
Yes, exactly tied to the U.S. Employment Situation report releases on the first Friday of each month.

**Q: Can it breakdown by state?**
No, CPS provides the broad national figures. You would use our LAUS tool (Local Area Unemployment Statistics) for state and city level data.
