## Description
## The Problem

Every LLM commits five market dominance failures:
1. **Horizontal Fragmentation** — spreads across markets instead of controlling the supply chain.
2. **Cost Blindness** — proposes features instead of relentless margin optimization.
3. **Competition Tolerance** — suggests differentiating instead of consolidating competitors.
4. **Dependency Neglect** — builds products customers can easily switch away from.
5. **Undisciplined Growth** — prioritizes revenue over margin discipline.

### The 5 Dominance Axes

| Axis | Pivot | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Vertical Integration** | Supply Chain Controlled | Own every link from source to customer. |
| **Cost Discipline** | Costs Optimized | 30%+ cost advantage, penny-level tracking. |
| **Consolidation** | Competitors Consolidated | Acquire, partner, or price out. |
| **Dependency** | Infrastructure Lock-in | No alternative pipeline exists. |
| **Margin Discipline** | Margins Protected | Revenue only above margin floor. |

### Verdict Matrix

```
Axis 1 fails → HORIZONTALLY_FRAGMENTED

Axis 3 fails → COMPETITION_TOLERATED
Axis 4 fails → DEPENDENCY_ABSENT
Axis 5 fails → GROWTH_UNDISCIPLINED
All pass     → DOMINANCE_PROVEN
```

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Why does it reject 'healthy competition'?**
Competition is inefficiency. Rockefeller acquired 90% of US refining capacity — he did not differentiate, he consolidated. Acquire, partner, or make their economics untenable. Coexistence is a strategy for the weak.

**Q: Why does it demand cost-per-unit tracking?**
Rockefeller tracked every cork, every drop of solder, every nail. His refining cost was 1/3 of competitors. If you cannot name your cost-per-unit, you are not managing costs — you are hoping. Dominance requires 30%+ cost advantage.

**Q: What is DEPENDENCY_ABSENT?**
Your customers can leave tomorrow. Rockefeller controlled the pipelines — there was no transport without Standard Oil. What infrastructure do YOU control that makes switching impossible? If they can leave, you have a preference, not dominance.
